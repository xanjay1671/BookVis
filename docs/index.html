Books Written Over Time by Genre, Normalized

This streamgraph shows the relative popularity of each genre over time. The most significant trend visible is the shrinking grey classics genre, which makes sense - classics aren't written, it's a genre a book ages into. place.

More interestingly, the red romance and green contemporary have grown from almost nothing to a substantial portion of books written.

Links Between Genres

This is a chord diagram depicting how genres are related to each other. Each book is classified as several genres, and as such, similarities and overlaps between genres can be found. For instance, there is a large overlap between fantasy and romance, however there are very few commons books between nonfiction and historical fiction.

Average Rating by Genre

A quick look at the rating distributions of genres. This graph is categorized with the raw input genre data, as opposed to a shortened genre list many of the other visualizations use, to better analyze outliers.

On the positive side, the top three highest rated genres are three varieties of illustrations. Additionally, young-adult is the genre with the fifth highest average - together indicating a bias of younger readers to be more generous with ratings.
